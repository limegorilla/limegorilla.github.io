So how does this all work?

Well it's quite simple. Ish. So this guide (per se) is here to help. First, you are going to need the lesson-specific key I should have given you. Without it, you will not have access to my documents. But do not worry - if you loose the passcode. I can print off a new one... For a price. $$
Once you have the key, select your subject below, where a dialogue box will pop up. This is where the key comes in. Once you have entered it (It iS CaSE SeNSiTIVE) you will be taken to my Google Drive. This is where each lessons files are kept. The organisational structure is simple, and is as follows:

  • Subject
  • Academic Year
  • Term
  • Files

    • Each document is created with the same theme (which I have to admit, has really started to get boring, but it is visually appealling and universal across classes sooo...) But each doument is created per topic that is done in class. Just select the document you wish to view and it will open up in a new window.
    PLEASE NOTE!
    While this webpage is being created, due to my (probably cynical) worries about security and the way this website is being created, documents might not be fully up to date. I am trying to minimise this as quick as possible, but at a very maximum they will be a week behind. That should not last long however. You will also find that this entire webpage (limegorilla.github.io) is not as stable as I would like. There are many buggy easter eggs around the page, and if you have a few minites, you may want to find some! Or not. Either way, if the webpage starts to really slow down, or just outright crashes because I missed a closing tag, a reload should fix it. Press F5 on your keyboard (loacated at the top) and the page should reload. On mobile, open your browsers quick settings and hit "reload"
    As well as the (possibly) buggy page, you may find that it is likely to change from time to time. That's just me updating it. If you are intested in what I am doing, you can visit the thread at the change log on the Development portion of the site.
    See you at school!
